Everton Football Club

Nil Satis Nisi Optimum

And if you know your history, it's enough to make your heart go Wooooooaaaaaa

In case you don't, start catching up!
  1. 1871: The football club St. Domingo's F.C. was formed, taking its name from St. Domingo Methodist New Connexion Chapel located on Breckfield Road North, Everton, Liverpool.
  2. 1879: Amid growing interest from people not associated with the church, the team name was changed to Everton Football Club.
  3. 1888: Everton became founder members of the Football League, finishing eighth the first season and second the following.
  4. 1890-91: Everton win the Football League Championship for the first time.
  5. 1892: Following politically charged disputes with the stadium owner, Everton abandoned Anfield stadium and moved across Stanley Park to Goodison Park. After failing to exploit Everton FC, the stadium owner created Liverpool FC, a cheap knockoff of a proper club and second choice from its very roots.
  6. 1906: Everton win the FA Cup for the first time.
  7. 1927-28: Dixie Dean scores 60 goals in one season while playing for Everton. This remarkable achievement remains a record and is widely considered one of the greatest individual accomplishments in English football history.
  8. 1929-30: Everton finish last in the first division and are relegated to the second division.
  9. 1930-31: Everton finish first in the second divison, immediately returning to the first division.
  10. 1931-32: Everton win their fourth league title in the first division.
  11. 1933: Everton win the FA cup again.
  12. 1938-39: Everton win the league title again.
  13. 1940s and 50s: The Football League was hit hard by WWII, and once the war was over the strong Everton side from 1939 was split up leaving a much weaker team in its place.
  14. 1951: After a poor season, Everton are relegated to the second division for only the second time in their history.
  15. 1954: Everton are promoted to the first division after finishing as the runners-up. The final match of the season decided promotion when Everton beat Oldham Athletic away 0-4.
  16. 1960s: Regarded as the golden era by many fans. Harry Catterick took charge of Everton in 1961 and the club was soon referred to as the "School of Science" in the tradition of the Everton team in the 1920s who were first given this name. Everton won the League Championship and the Charity Shield twice (1963 and 1970 for both) and the FA Cup once (1966)
  17. 1981-1987: The Howard Kendall Era. Howard Kendall, a former player from the 1960's "golden era" Everton side, returned as manager and guided Everton to a host of silverware. Honours Won: FA Cup (1984), Charity Shield (1984), European Cup Winners Cup (1985), League Championship (1985), Charity Shield (1985), Charity Shield Shared (1986), League Championship (1987)

Though Everton has gone without silverware for many years, the fans show an unwavering support for their club. This has been especially evident during back to back seasons barely avoiding relegation followed by a glimmer of hope in the 2023/24 season, only to be dashed by a Premier League record 10 point deduction for a Profit and Sustainability violation.
